Receita de Carne Assada


Ingredientes
  • 1kg Acem
  • 2 colheres de sopa de vinagre
  • 4 dentes de alho
  • Cominho
  • Sal
  • 1 colher de óleo
  • 2 folhas de louro
  • 1 cebola picada
  • 5 colheres de sopa de Shoyo
  • Pimenta branca
  • Noz moscada

Modo de preparo
  1. Em um recipiente, misture todos os temperos, acrescente a carne e deixe marinar por 30 minutos.
  2. Aqueça a panela de pressão, acrescente o óleo, a carne com o molho do tempero e tampe a panela.
  3. Quando a panela atingir a pressão, abaixe o fogo e deixe cozinhar por 1 hora.
  4. Está pronto para servir!

Nearly 15% of Americans don’t believe climate change is real, study finds

A recent study from the University of Michigan has unveiled that nearly 15% of Americans remain skeptical about the reality of climate change, highlighting the deeply divided perspectives on this issue across the country. This skepticism is notably more prevalent in the central and southern regions of the United States, with a significant correlation found between political affiliation and belief in climate science; Republican voters are generally less convinced of climate change compared to their Democratic counterparts. The research employed artificial intelligence to analyze over 7.4 million tweets related to climate change, categorizing them based on their stance towards the phenomenon, revealing a substantial number of posts denying climate change's existence.

The influence of public figures, particularly Donald Trump, on shaping public opinion against the acceptance of climate change has been significant, as demonstrated by the engagement with his tweets that dismissed climate science and reports. This pattern of denial and skepticism is not isolated, with similar sentiments echoed across various platforms and discussions, further complicating the battle against climate misinformation. Despite the high denial rates in certain areas, there are also regions, especially along the coasts, where acceptance of climate change is more widespread, reflecting a complex mosaic of beliefs across the United States.

The study also sheds light on the ethical considerations and potential biases inherent in using artificial intelligence for research purposes, especially in sensitive areas like climate change. While AI offers a powerful tool for processing large volumes of data, the necessity for human oversight to mitigate biases and inaccuracies is emphasized. Moreover, the role of social media platforms in curbing misinformation and fostering a more informed public discourse on critical issues like climate change is highlighted as a pivotal aspect of addressing the broader challenges of knowledge vulnerability and misinformation in the digital age.
